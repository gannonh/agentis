# Agentis Strategy

## Target problem

Knowledge workers can use agent tools from Anthropic and OpenAI, but those options are tied to specific frontier models and their pricing. For small businesses especially, sustained agent use is often cost-prohibitive. The crux is widening: AI spend is rising faster than teams can tie it to measurable ROI.

## Our approach

Bring knowledge workers agent-driven workflows comparable to what engineers already have—through an open, self-hostable platform with model optionality (including open-source models) so teams aren't locked to a single frontier lab or priced out of sustained, long-running agent use.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** Knowledge worker (horizontal)—marketers, salespeople, business analysts, and similar roles. They're hiring Agentis to delegate a large share of their workload to autonomous agents and operate with a team of agents that helps them do their job.

## Key metrics

- **Productivity per active user** — agent-completed work units (runs, artifacts, delegated tasks) per knowledge worker per week *(draft — operationalize)*
- **Headcount leverage** — ratio of agent-attributed output to human intervention required; proxy for "more output with fewer people" *(draft — operationalize)*
- **Active agents per user** — configured agents regularly used per account *(draft)*
- **Run quality pass rate** — share of runs meeting evaluation rubrics without rework *(draft)*

## Tracks

### Native tooling

Workspace file tools, sandboxed execution, web search, and other Agentis-owned capabilities that let agents act on durable working state without leaving the platform.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Knowledge-work agents need safe, inspectable native actions—not just chat—to match the depth engineers get in a dev environment.

### Third-party integrations

Connecting external software and data sources so agents can operate across the tools knowledge workers already use.

_Why it serves the approach:_ General knowledge work is inherently cross-tool; integrations extend agent reach beyond native tooling alone.

### UX and UI fidelity

Workbench UI, thread experience, artifact workspaces, and operational surfaces that make agent work legible, calm, and trustworthy.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Knowledge workers won't delegate large portions of their workload to agents they can't understand, inspect, or trust.

### Agent evaluations and performance improvements

Rubrics, quality signals, command-center visibility, and iteration loops that make agent output measurable and improvable over time.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Sustained delegation requires confidence that agent work is good—not just fast—especially when ROI is the business bet.
